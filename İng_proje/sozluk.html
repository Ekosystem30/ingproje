    [ mI, m& ] (adjective.) 12th century. From Middle English my, apocopated form of min, myn, from Old English m?n (“my, mine”), from Proto-Germanic *m?naz (“my, mine”, pron.), genitive of Proto-Germanic *ek (“I”). Cognate with West Frisian myn (“my”), Afrikaans my (“my”), Dutch mijn (“my”), German mein (“my”). More at me.
